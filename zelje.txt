KAKO JE SVE POČELO: 
Kako je sve počelo / How It All Started
Srpski

Moja priča sa Malim princom nije počela u osnovnoj školi, kada je on bio samo deo obavezne lektire. Prava veza se rodila znatno kasnije, 1996. godine, tokom studija u studentskom domu „Košutnjak“. Tada sam dobila primerak od svoje tadašnje cimerke, a današnje kume i „sestre“ Svetlane. Od tog trenutka, to je postala moja omiljena knjiga koja me je pratila na svim mojim putovanjima.

Preokret se dogodio 2008. godine na letovanju u Turskoj. Dok sam po ko zna koji put čitala svoj primerak na plaži, poverila sam rođaki Danieli jednu misao: „Ovu knjigu znam skoro napamet. Mogla bih da kupim primerak na francuskom, čisto da malo obnovim jezik koji sam zaboravila.“

Tog novembra 2008. godine, dobila sam prvi primerak na francuskom jeziku. Ta dva izdanja – na srpskom i francuskom – dugo su stajala usamljena. Iako sam u glavi imala želju da ih imam na još više jezika, ideja je čekala pravi trenutak. Sve se promenilo tokom jedne posete Segedinu. U tamošnjoj knjižari, potražila sam Malim princa na mađarskom, a izašla sam sa izdanjima na mađarskom, španskom i rumunskom jeziku.

Od tog dana, sve ostalo je istorija koja danas broji preko 460 primeraka na više od 330 jezika i dijalekata.

---

English

I can’t say it all began back in elementary school when The Little Prince was just a mandatory school assignment. The true connection was formed much later, in 1996, while I was a student living in the "Košutnjak" dormitory. I received a copy from my roommate at the time, who is today my godmother and "sister," Svetlana. Since then, it has been my favorite book, traveling with me wherever I went.

The turning point came in 2008 during a summer holiday in Turkey. As I was reading my copy on the beach for the umpteenth time, I shared a thought with my cousin Daniela: "I practically know this book by heart. Maybe I should buy a copy in French, just to brush up on the language I’ve forgotten."

In November 2008, I received my first copy in French. For a long time, those two editions—Serbian and French—stood alone on my shelf. Although I harbored a wish to have the book in even more languages, the idea remained just a thought until a visit to Szeged. In a local bookstore, I asked for The Little Prince in Hungarian, and I walked out with copies in Hungarian, Spanish, and Romanian.

From that day on, the rest is history—a history that now encompasses over 460 copies in more than 330 languages and dialects.


ZA DEO O KOLEKCIJI:
O kolekciji / About the Collection
Srpski

Ono što je počelo kao lična potraga za jednim francuskim izdanjem, preraslo je u sistematičnu kolekciju koja danas broji preko 469 knjiga. Moja misija nije samo sakupljanje brojeva, već dokumentovanje neverovatne lingvističke raznolikosti kojom svet progovara kroz reči Sent Egziperija. Kolekcija danas obuhvata izdanja na više od 338 svetskih jezika i dijalekata.

Ova riznica je, pre svega, svedočanstvo o divnim ljudima i prijateljstvima. Mnoge knjige na mojim policama stigle su kao darovi dragih prijatelja koji su „Malog princa“ tražili na svojim putovanjima širom sveta. Posebnu zahvalnost dugujem Vladimiru (Mrkiju), Milošu (Aždaji), Vladi, Radmili, Dejani, Oliveri, Mariji, Filipu, Dušanu, Tamari, Nadi, kao i Mireli koja mi je nesebično poslala svoj dragoceni primerak čak sa Farskih ostrva. Zahvaljujući njima, moja polica danas miriše na Japan, Maltu, Zanzibar, Grčku i ceo naš region.

Drugi, jednako važan deo kolekcije, čine knjige do kojih sam došla putem razmene sa drugim kolekcionarima. Kontakti sa ljudima kao što su Jen, Mertxe, Jean-Mark, Alfons, Elena, Kastaneda, Melih, Gerhard, Melisa, Laura, Slavek, Martina, Evelina, Kuncoro, Ilyas, Viviana, Adela, Juan Carlos, Maria, Mary, Ferenc, Ananya, Saosoong, Supoj, Christoforos, Jose, Mark, Jordi, kao i mnogi drugi prijatelji, prerasli su u stalnu komunikaciju i zajedničku misiju. Bez truda svih ovih prijatelja da pronađu retka izdanja iz Indonezije, Meksika, Španije, Litvanije ili Švajcarske, moja kolekcija danas ne bi bila ovako bogata i živa.

---

English

What began as a personal search for a single French edition has grown into a systematic collection that today includes over 469 books. My mission is not merely about accumulating numbers, but about documenting the incredible linguistic diversity through which the world speaks using Saint-Exupéry’s words. The collection features editions in more than 338 world languages and rare dialects.

This treasury is also a testament to wonderful people and friendships. Many of the books on my shelves arrived as gifts from dear friends who, knowing my passion, sought out The Little Prince during their travels—from Japan, Malta, and Zanzibar to Greece, Macedonia, Republika Srpska, and Croatia. I owe a special debt of gratitude to Vladimir (Mrki), Miloš (Aždaja), Vlada, Radmila, Dejana, Olivera, Marija, Filip, Dušan, Tamara, Nada, and to Mirela, who selflessly sent me her precious copy all the way from the Faroe Islands.

A special segment is dedicated to books received through exchanges with fellow collectors worldwide. Connections with people like Jen, Mertxe, Jean-Mark, Alfons, Elena, Kastaneda, Melih, Gerhard, Melisa, Laura, Slavek, Martina, Evelina, Kuncoro, Ilyas, Viviana, Adela, Juan Carlos, Maria, Mary, Ferenc, Ananya, Saosoong, Supoj, Christoforos, Jose, Mark, Jordi, and many others, have grown into ongoing communication and a shared mission. Without the effort of all these friends to find rare editions from Indonesia, Mexico, Spain, Lithuania, Switzerland, and beyond, my collection would not be nearly as rich or diverse today.

PODNASLOV KOD "O KOLEKCIJI":
ČUVAR DIGITALNE BIBLIOTEKE
Posebno mjesto u ovoj priči, i u svakom koraku razvoja moje zbirke, zauzima moj prijatelj CHUPCKO. On nije bio samo inicijator odlaska u Segedin, gdje je sve zapravo i počelo, već je neko čiji se "prstići" nalaze u skoro svakom dijelu ove kolekcije. CHUPCKO mi je otvorio vrata svijeta digitalne razmjene, pronašavši grupe i ljude sa istom strašću, što je pokrenulo moje prve međunarodne razmjene. Mnoge dragocjene primjerke – od Zanzibara i Japana do Malte – donijeli su mi upravo njegovi prijatelji i kolege koje je on nesebično animirao da mi pomognu. Uz to, on je idejni tvorac i tehnički stub sajta koji upravo gledate; on me je naučio kako da bilježim svaki novi primjerak i pomaže mi da ova digitalna biblioteka raste i ostane organizovana.

English

Guardian of the Digital Library
A very special place in this story, and in every step of my collection’s growth, belongs to my friend CHUPCKO. He wasn’t just the one who initiated that first trip to Szeged where it all truly began; he is someone whose "fingerprints" can be found in almost every part of this journey. CHUPCKO opened the doors to the world of digital exchange for me, discovering groups and people who share this same passion, which sparked my first international trades. Many precious editions—from Zanzibar and Japan to Malta—were brought to me by his friends and colleagues, whom he selflessly encouraged to help. Furthermore, he is the creator and technical backbone of the website you are currently visiting; he taught me how to catalog every new copy and continues to help me keep this digital library organized.
